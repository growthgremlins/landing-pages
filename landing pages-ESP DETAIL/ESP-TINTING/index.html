They differ in how they reject heat. Dyed uses pigments that darken your glass and primarily block visible light. Carbon uses carbon particles to reject more heat and won't fade to purple over time. Ceramic uses non-conductive ceramic particles to reject infrared heat — the heat you actually feel — without affecting GPS or cell signals. Nano-Ceramic stacks multiple engineered nano-particle layers for the highest possible heat and IR rejection in a single film.
California requires front-side windows to allow more than 70% of light through (so any film on the fronts has to be very light). Rear-side and back windows have no darkness limit — you can go as dark as you like. The windshield permits non-reflective tint only on the top 4 inches (above the AS-1 line). Drivers with a qualifying condition can apply for a medical exemption for darker front tint. We'll walk you through the legal options during your consultation and keep your install street-legal.
A full sedan typically takes 2 to 3 hours. SUVs, trucks, and vehicles with larger rear windows can run 3 to 4 hours. We recommend keeping windows up for 3 to 5 days while the film fully cures and the adhesive bonds to the glass. You may notice a slight haze or small water pockets for the first week or two — that's the curing process, not a defect, and it dries completely clear.
